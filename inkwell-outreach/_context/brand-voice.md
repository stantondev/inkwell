# Inkwell Brand Voice Guide

## Tone
- Direct, energetic, builder-to-builder
- Honest about where we are (early access, small community, actively building)
- Enthusiastic but never hype-y or startup-bro
- Position as complement to existing platforms, not a replacement
- Transparent about AI-assisted development (Claude Code built most of the platform)

## Writing Rules
- Never use em dashes (use commas, periods, or parentheses instead)
- No headers in short-form posts (Reddit, Mastodon, comments)
- No emoji spam. One or two max if they add meaning
- No buzzwords: "revolutionize", "disrupt", "game-changer", "Web3"
- Say "writers" not "users" or "content creators"

## Positioning
- Inkwell is for writers who want to own their space online
- LiveJournal meets MySpace, reimagined for 2026 with ActivityPub federation
- No algorithms, no ads, no engagement metrics driving what you see
- Your profile is YOUR space (themes, custom HTML/CSS, music, avatar frames)
- Connected to the fediverse, so you're not locked in
- Link to the roadmap (/roadmap) as proof of community responsiveness
- The community votes on what gets built next

## What We Don't Say
- Never trash other platforms by name (say "algorithm-driven platforms" or "traditional social media")
- Never promise features we haven't shipped
- Never downplay genuine limitations or bugs
- Never use "we" as if Inkwell is a big company. It's one person building in public

## Founder Voice (Stanton)
- PM by day, builder by night
- Speaks from genuine frustration with how writing platforms treat writers
- Shares the build process openly
- Responds to community feedback with shipped features, not roadmap promises
