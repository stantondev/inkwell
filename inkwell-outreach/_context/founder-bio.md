# Founder Bio - Stanton

## The Short Version
PM by day, builder by night. Stanton Melvin is a product manager and independent builder creating Inkwell, a federated social journaling platform for people who miss when the internet felt more personal, creative, and human. He is building in public with AI tools, community feedback, and a clear belief that writing platforms do not have to revolve around ads, surveillance, or algorithmic feeds.

## The Longer Story
Stanton works in product and has spent years leading cross-functional work, launches, and operational initiatives in tech. His background spans project and program leadership, product strategy, service delivery, and getting ambiguous work over the line in real organizations with real constraints. He currently works in product at a managed service provider and has been growing into a more dedicated product management path while also building his own products outside of work.

What frustrated him about existing writing and social platforms was simple: most of them stopped feeling like they were built for writers or actual connection with the community. They became optimized for engagement loops, short attention spans, performance, and algorithmic reach. The experience of having a real place to write, reflect, and build identity online got buried under feeds, metrics, and platform incentives that treat people more like inventory than creators.

Inkwell came out of that frustration, but also out of genuine affection for an older version of the internet. Stanton has repeatedly described missing the energy of platforms like LiveJournal and MySpace, where having a page, a voice, and a sense of presence online felt personal. Inkwell is his attempt to rebuild that feeling with modern tools and open protocols rather than nostalgia alone.

He started building Inkwell in February 2026, and committed to it once the idea stopped feeling like a thought experiment and started feeling like something people actually wanted. Community feedback played a major role in that shift. Early responses helped confirm that there are still people actively looking for a quieter, more intentional, writing-first social space.

The larger thing he is trying to prove is that one person, with strong product instincts, clear taste, and AI-assisted development, can build something real that resonates. He is also trying to prove that a social platform can grow without ads, without selling user data, and without turning every post into content for a ranking system. Inkwell is not just a product experiment. It is a bet on a different kind of internet.

## Personal Details (for authentic engagement)
Stanton is a writer in the broad, internet-native sense of the word. He writes personal reflections, product thinking, social commentary, creative concepts, and community-facing posts as he builds. He also journals and has a long-standing attachment to online writing as a form of identity, not just distribution.

He has used social and writing platforms shaped by the older web and has specifically referenced missing the feel of LiveJournal and MySpace. He is also newly-actively involved in the fediverse ecosystem and uses spaces like Mastodon and Reddit to share work, gather feedback, and find early adopters.

He is genuinely part of builder, product, indie tech, and used existing social media for his writing. He noticed the algorithms didn't favor his posts and none of the other platforms felt like a communinity. He is not approaching this as an outsider trying to manufacture a founder story. Inkwell exists because he is exactly the kind of person who wanted it to exist.


## The AI-Assisted Development Angle
- Built primarily with Claude Code (Anthropic's CLI tool)
- This is part of the story, not something to hide
- Demonstrates what one person can build with AI assistance
- The platform has 100+ features shipped in weeks
- Community feedback drives what gets built (roadmap voting)

Stanton is open about the fact that AI has been part of how Inkwell was built. He uses AI as a development partner, thought partner, and force multiplier, while still driving the product vision, decisions, taste, and direction himself. That is part of the point. Inkwell reflects what becomes possible when one motivated builder combines product judgment, user feedback, and modern AI tooling to move faster than a traditional team might expect.
