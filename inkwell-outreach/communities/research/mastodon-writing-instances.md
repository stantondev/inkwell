# Mastodon Writing Instances Research

Last updated: 2026-03-09

## Why Mastodon Matters for Inkwell

Inkwell already federates via ActivityPub. Mastodon users can follow Inkwell writers at @username@inkwell.social. This makes Mastodon communities a natural audience: they already value decentralization, user ownership, and no-algorithm feeds. These are Inkwell's core values too.

## Writer-Focused Instances

### writing.exchange
- **Focus**: Poets, authors, and every kind of writer
- **Size**: Small, intentional community
- **Running since**: 2017
- **Vibe**: "An exchange" of words and ideas. Active participation encouraged.
- **Moderation**: @matt and @wordsmith. Backed by Musing Studio / Write.as
- **Self-promotion**: Not explicitly banned. The culture is sharing your writing and process. Sharing a platform you built for writers would likely be welcome if done authentically.
- **Onboarding**: Encourages introductions. "Communities like these work best when they're filled with strangers who talk to each other."
- **Financial model**: Community donations via Open Collective
- **Best approach**: Join, introduce yourself as a writer who built a writing platform. Share your writing there. Mention Inkwell naturally when relevant. The fact that Inkwell federates means writing.exchange users can follow Inkwell writers without leaving their instance.
- **URL**: https://writing.exchange

### bookstodon.com
- **Focus**: Bookish folks (readers, writers, reviewers, publishers)
- **Size**: Launched late 2022, growing community
- **Vibe**: Indie/literary-friendly. Broad but welcoming.
- **Self-promotion**: No ad system. Promotion through posts, threads, hashtags, and genuine participation.
- **Best approach**: More reader-focused than writer-focused. Good for Inkwell writers who review books or write about reading. Less direct fit for platform outreach.
- **URL**: https://bookstodon.com

### me.dm (Medium's instance)
- **Focus**: Medium authors, publications, and readers finding a fediverse home
- **Size**: Backed by Medium, likely larger user base
- **Vibe**: Professional writers, journalism-adjacent
- **Self-promotion**: Likely more tolerant given the Medium-writer audience
- **Best approach**: Interesting strategically. Medium writers frustrated with Medium's algorithm are already here AND already in the fediverse. They're primed for an alternative. But posting from Medium's own instance about a Medium competitor might feel awkward.
- **URL**: https://me.dm

### zirk.us
- **Focus**: "Readers, writers, thinkers, artists, academics, enthusiasts in arts and humanities"
- **Size**: Small
- **Vibe**: Academic/literary
- **Best approach**: Good for longer-form writing discussion. Inkwell's Article-type federation (FEP-b2b8) is especially relevant here.

## General Mastodon Instances Worth Being On

### mastodon.social
- The flagship instance. Largest user base. General purpose.
- Good for reach but less community feeling.

### hackers.town / fosstodon.org
- Tech/FOSS focused. Good for the "built with Claude Code" and "ActivityPub federation" angles.
- Self-promotion of open/federated projects is generally welcome.

## Mastodon Engagement Best Practices

1. **Join an instance that matches your identity**: writing.exchange is the obvious choice
2. **Introduce yourself with #introduction**: Mastodon culture encourages detailed intro posts
3. **Use CW (Content Warnings) for self-promotion**: Some instances expect it. When in doubt, use CW
4. **Boost others generously**: Mastodon culture values amplification over self-promotion
5. **Thread longer content**: Break into threaded posts, don't dump walls of text
6. **Alt text on images**: Mastodon communities care about accessibility
7. **No algorithm means timing matters**: Post when your audience is active (varies by instance timezone)
8. **Cross-instance follows work**: You don't need to BE on writing.exchange to follow/interact with people there

## Strategic Insight

The killer angle for Mastodon communities: "Your Mastodon followers can already follow Inkwell writers. We're part of the same network." This isn't a pitch for a new silo. It's showing that Inkwell extends the fediverse for long-form writing. That resonates deeply with fediverse values.

## Recommended Priority

1. **writing.exchange** (join, participate, be a writer there)
2. **Mastodon generally** (post from whatever instance, use hashtags)
3. **fosstodon.org** (for the tech/builder angle)
4. **bookstodon.com** (secondary, more reader-focused)

## Competitor Context: Ghost + Fediverse

Ghost (blogging platform) announced ActivityPub support in 2024 and started federating in July 2024. This is both competition and validation: it proves the fediverse-for-writers space is real. Inkwell's differentiator: it's a social platform with profiles, stamps, guestbooks, circles, not just a blog with federation bolted on.
